# Tutorials

Each tutorial is runnable. Pick a scenario close to something you're actually working on and follow along. The goal is not for you to reproduce the example. The goal is for you to get a feel for the moves so you can apply them to your own situation.

Every tutorial opens with a set of **questions to sit with** before you start. These are not comprehension questions. They are the questions the tutorial is going to put pressure on. Think about your own answers first. The tool is only useful to the extent it changes how you answer them.

## Start here

- [**Getting started**](getting-started.md) — about twenty minutes. Walk an idea through opportunity, objective, assumption, and experiment. The full loop at shallow depth.

## Go deeper

- [**The full discovery loop**](full-discovery-loop.md) — about an hour. Runs a realistic discovery cycle from ambiguous signal through shipped learning. This is the one to do on a quiet afternoon.

## Crossing from discovery to development

- [**Crossing the handoff**](crossing-the-handoff.md) — about thirty minutes. Takes a validated idea out of `docs/discovery/` and turns it into a scoped project, a ready story, a grounded spec, and an ADR under `docs/development/`. Run it on the output of the full discovery loop, or on a real validated idea of your own.

## Development

- [**Getting started (develop)**](develop-getting-started.md) — about thirty minutes. Take a ready story through assess, spec, ADR, test plan, build, self-review, and PR. The engineer's parallel to the discovery getting-started.

## Focused practice

Shorter, scenario-specific tutorials. Each targets one move.

- [**Surfacing assumptions**](surfacing-assumptions.md) — how to find what an idea is actually betting on, especially the beliefs that feel too obvious to name.
- [**Designing experiments**](designing-experiments.md) — how to turn a risky belief into the cheapest test that would settle it, and what to do when the results are mixed.
- [**Critiquing an idea**](critiquing-an-idea.md) — how to stress-test an idea through multiple personas and frameworks without drifting into negativity theater.

## A note on pace

These tutorials are written to be interrupted. Stop when a question is interesting. Come back when you have something real to try it on. The opportunity graph is supposed to be a record of actual thinking. A tutorial graph you abandoned still taught you how the tool moves. That is the whole goal of the tutorials.
